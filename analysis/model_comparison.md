# Model Comparison

| Model | Dependent Variable | Independent Variable(s) | R² | Significant Variables | Business Usefulness | Limitations |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Simple Regression 1 | Monthly Sales | Marketing Spend | 0.7819 | Marketing Spend (p < 0.05) | Useful for understanding impact of marketing on sales | Uses only one predictor |
| Simple Regression 2 | Monthly Sales | Footfall | 0.7363 | Footfall (p < 0.05) | Useful for understanding impact of customer visits on sales | Uses only one predictor |
| Multiple Regression | Monthly Sales | Marketing Spend, Footfall | 0.7819 | Marketing Spend, Footfall (p < 0.05) | Better for decision-making because it considers multiple factors | Does not include all possible business factors |

## Conclusion

The Multiple Regression model is preferred because it considers more than one predictor while maintaining a high R² value. Both Marketing Spend and Footfall are statistically significant and positively affect Monthly Sales.
