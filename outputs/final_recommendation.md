# Final Recommendation

## 1. Which factors appear most strongly associated with monthly sales?

Based on the regression analysis, the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales are:

- Footfall
- Marketing Spend

Both variables were statistically significant and showed positive relationships with monthly sales.

The multiple regression model achieved an R² of approximately 0.7819, indicating that these variables explain about 78% of the variation in monthly sales.

---

## 2. Which variables should leadership focus on?

Leadership should focus primarily on:

### Footfall
- Footfall has a strong positive effect on monthly sales.
- Increasing customer visits can directly improve sales performance.

### Marketing Spend
- Marketing spend also has a positive relationship with sales.
- Effective marketing campaigns can help attract more customers and increase revenue.

---

## 3. Which variables should not be over-interpreted?

Variables that were not included in the final regression model or were not statistically significant should not be over-interpreted.

Business decisions should be based mainly on variables that showed strong statistical evidence in the regression results.

---

## 4. What business action would you recommend?

Recommended actions:

1. Increase investment in effective marketing campaigns.
2. Implement strategies that improve store traffic and customer visits.
3. Monitor both marketing spend and footfall regularly.
4. Use the regression model to forecast future sales and support planning decisions.

These actions are supported by the positive coefficients and significant p-values found in the regression analysis.

---

## 5. What risks or limitations should leadership keep in mind?

- The model explains about 78% of sales variation, but not 100%.
- Other factors may influence sales, including competition, economic conditions, customer preferences, and seasonal effects.
- Future business conditions may differ from historical data.

Therefore, regression results should be used as decision-support tools rather than the sole basis for decisions.

---

## 6. Why does regression show association but not automatically prove causation?

Regression identifies relationships between variables but does not prove that one variable directly causes another.

For example, higher marketing spend is associated with higher sales, but other factors may also contribute to sales growth.

Additional experiments or controlled studies would be required to establish true causation.

---

## Final Conclusion

The Multiple Regression Model is the preferred model because it provides strong explanatory power (R² = 0.7819) and incorporates multiple important business factors.

The analysis suggests that increasing marketing effectiveness and improving footfall are the most practical ways to increase monthly sales. Leadership should focus on these areas while considering the limitations of the model and monitoring other business factors.
